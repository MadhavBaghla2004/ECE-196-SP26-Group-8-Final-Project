<!-- PROBLEM -->
<div class="content-glass" id="problem">
  <h2 style="color:#ffffff; font-size:1.7rem; font-weight:800; text-align:center; margin:0 0 24px; letter-spacing:0.5px; text-shadow:0 2px 8px rgba(0,0,0,0.6);">
    Improving Seed Germination in Hydroponic Systems
  </h2>

  <h3 style="color:#ffffff; font-size:1.15rem; font-weight:700; margin:0 0 8px; text-shadow:0 1px 4px rgba(0,0,0,0.5);">Problem Definition</h3>
  <p style="font-size:1.1rem; color:rgba(255,255,255,0.88); line-height:1.8; margin:0 0 20px; text-shadow:0 1px 3px rgba(0,0,0,0.4);">
    Hydroponic growers often face high failure rates during seed germination due to difficulties in controlling critical environmental factors such as temperature and humidity, especially when using substrates like rock wool. These challenges increase the complexity of growing plants from seeds and create a significant barrier to entry for beginners in hydroponics.
  </p>

  <h3 style="color:#ffffff; font-size:1.15rem; font-weight:700; margin:0 0 8px; text-shadow:0 1px 4px rgba(0,0,0,0.5);">Motivation</h3>
  <p style="font-size:1.1rem; color:rgba(255,255,255,0.88); line-height:1.8; margin:0 0 20px; text-shadow:0 1px 3px rgba(0,0,0,0.4);">
    Last summer, Nathaniel built a hydroponics tower from scratch, assembling components, 3D printing connectors, and preparing multiple plant varieties for germination; however, despite his efforts, the seeds failed due to environmental conditions, leaving the system unused. While existing products such as seed trays with domes, heat pads, and lighting aim to improve germination, they still require significant user knowledge, monitoring, and manual adjustments, making them difficult for beginners. This highlights an opportunity to develop a more automated system that minimizes user input and intelligently controls environmental conditions, reducing failure rates and making hydroponics more accessible.
  </p>

  <h3 style="color:#ffffff; font-size:1.15rem; font-weight:700; margin:0 0 8px; text-shadow:0 1px 4px rgba(0,0,0,0.5);">Existing Solutions</h3>
  <p style="font-size:1.1rem; color:rgba(255,255,255,0.88); line-height:1.8; margin:0 0 24px; text-shadow:0 1px 3px rgba(0,0,0,0.4);">
    Current hydroponic solutions attempt to simplify seed germination but still rely heavily on manual intervention, requiring users to understand and maintain optimal conditions such as temperature, humidity, and nutrient levels. Many systems use simple greenhouse-style trays with misting setups, which lack adaptability for different seed types and environmental conditions, leaving room for improvement in automation and customization. Relevant patents include US12543625B2 (Soil/Seed Monitoring System for Germination) and US4236350A (Seedling Tray/Greenhouse Assembly).
  </p>
